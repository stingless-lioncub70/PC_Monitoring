## 🔍 Understanding Hardware Telemetry

PC_Monitoring pulls raw data from your system hardware. CPU telemetry shows how hard your processor works during tasks. High CPU usage suggests a heavy workload. GPU telemetry tracks graphics cards. This matters if you play games or edit videos. RAM telemetry shows how much memory your apps consume. If your RAM is full, your system feels slow. Disk telemetry tracks speed and capacity for your storage drives. Monitor these numbers to keep your system fast.

## 🛠️ Solving Common Problems

Most users encounter no issues during setup. If the app fails to start, restart your computer first. Ensure your version of Windows is up to date. The app requires some basic permissions to read hardware data. If you see blank charts, the app lacks access to your sensors. Run the app as an administrator to fix this. To do this, right-click the app icon and select Run as administrator. Contact your hardware manufacturer if sensors remain unresponsive.

## 📈 Tips For Better Monitoring

Use the dashboard during your heaviest tasks. Open a game or a large file to see how your hardware responds. Watch for spikes in CPU temperature. High heat reflects heavy load or dust buildup in your fans. Compare your idle numbers to your load numbers. This helps you identify hardware issues. Keep an eye on your disk space to prevent system errors. Use the real-time graphs to see how specific apps impact your speed.
